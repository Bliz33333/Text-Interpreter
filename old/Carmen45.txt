Acmen Septimius suos amores
Septimius, holding Acme, his love,

tenens in gremio ‘mea’ inquit ‘Acme,
in his lap says, “My Acme,

ni te perdite amo atque amare porro
unless I love you desperately, and am prepared to love you forward

omnes sum assidue paratus annos,
for all my years constantly,

quantum qui pote plurimum perire							
as much as he who is able to be very much desperately in love,

solus in Libya Indiaque tosta
may I come alone into Libya and roasted India

caesio veniam obvius leoni.’
to meet a grey-eyed lion.”

Hoc ut dixit, Amor sinistra ut ante
As he said this, Cupid sneezed his approval as before on the left

dextra sternuit approbationem.
on the right.

At Acme leviter caput reflectens								
But Acme, lightly bending her head back

et dulcis pueri ebrios ocellos
and, having kissed the drunk eyes of the sweet boy

illo purpureo ore suaviata,
with that wine-dark mouth,

‘Sic’ inquit ‘mea vita Septimille,
says, “In this way, my life, dear Septimius

huic uni domino usque serviamus,
let us serve this one master continuously,

ut multo mihi maior acriorque								
so that a passion much greater and sharper burns for me

ignis mollibus ardet in medullis.’
in my soft marrow.”

Hoc ut dixit, Amor sinistra ut ante
As she said this, Cupid sneezed his approval as before on the left

dextra sternuit approbationem.
on the right.

Nunc ab auspicio bono profecti
Now, having set out from this good omen,

mutuis animis amant amantur.							
they love (and) are loved with mutual spirits.

Unam Septimius misellus Acmen
Poor little Septimius prefers his one Acme

mavult quam Syrias Britanniasque:
to Syrias and Britains:

uno in Septimio fidelis Acme
loyal Acme finds her pleasures and desires

facit delicias libidinesque.
in her one Septimius.

Quis ullos homines beatiores								
Who has seen any happier people,

vidit, quis Venerem auspicatiorem?
who (has seen) a more well-omened love?
